Bird Flu

Easy steps that can help you protect your flock!

Housing

  • Should be in line with government guidance
  • Make sure no wild birds, mice or rats cannot access the area the birds live
  • Ensure no leaks in the roofs where the birds live

Cleaning top tips!

  • Regularly clean the areas where the birds have access
  • Disinfect equipment such as feeders and drinkers regularly

Food and water

  • Clean up spillages of feed
  • Feed wild garden birds separate from your captive birds
  • Don't let your birds share any nearby ponds

Clothing and footwear

  • Wash the footwear and clothes worn before you come into contact with your birds
  • Decrease cross contamination - wear separate shoes doing other activities

Infected birds have influenza viruses in their

  • Saliva
  • Mucous
  • Droppings

Avoid contact with domestic and wild birds

Report sick or unexpectedly dead birds to authorities

Self isolate if you have symptoms of influenza and seek medical advice

INFORMATION SHARING

With our innovative app, Bird Flu Watch, critical information is just a tap away. Designed for farmers, the app delivers real-time updates, expert tips, and actionable advice, ensuring that you have the tools you need to protect your animals and enhance your farm’s productivity.

RESEARCH

As a team, we conduct extensive research to stay at the forefront of avian influenza prevention. By sharing our findings and raising awareness, we empower farmers with the knowledge they need to safeguard their flocks and maintain healthy, productive farms.
